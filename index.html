Exporting / Moving Your Rdio Data

Rdio Farewell is the official export site, available for a limited time. Grab your data while it's still there!

Below is a list of additional options. Note that while the official export is probably your best way to get the data out of Rdio, you still have to get it into a new service; the links below can help.

Keep in mind when exporting to other services that some of them have limits on how many tracks they'll store (Spotify is 10,000 tracks, for instance).

  • Mooval (moves favorites, playlists to Spotify or Deezer...some people have reported issues)
  • Soundiiz Converter (moves playlists to a wide array of services)
  • Rdio >>> Spotify (move all your music to Spotify) by Jody Heavener
  • Spotificator (moves playlists to Spotify)
  • Move to Apple (Mac App that moves your playlists to Apple Music)
  • Rdio Export (exports playlists, comments, favorites, etc. to JSON) by Alex Gaynor
  • Export Favorite Albums (exports favorite albums to JSON) by Ian Gilman
  • Rdio Enhancer (Chrome extension, supports CSV export) by Mike Garrett
  • Ivy (Spotify playlist importer)
  • RIP Rdio An Rdio collection importer for Spotify by Doug Jones.
  • Spotify Album Importer (export your Rdio library directly to Spotify) by Ernest Leitch
  • Conservatorio (Export all of your Rdio metadata, including favorites, synced tracks, and playlists to a JSON file. Available as a Mac app or console application for Mac and Windows) by Aaron Bockover
  • Rdio Export (Python script to export your Rdio favorites and playlists to Google Play Music) by Ian McKellar
  • Retrieve Your Listening History (a post on how to retrieve your listening history using Chrome/cURL, and an undocumented API v1 method. outputs JSON)
  • rdio-history (on old but working Ruby tool (use Ruby 1.9.3) to retrieve your listening history using the same method. outputs plaintext.) by Ian Chan
  • RdioLastConvert (PHP script and instructions to convert your Rdio listening history JSON to Last.fm export format and import your history into Last.fm) by Rowcliffe Browne
  • RadioTide A tool to copy your favorites and collections from Rdio to Tidal
